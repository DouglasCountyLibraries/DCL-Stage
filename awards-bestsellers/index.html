Awards
James Beard Award
Awards presented annually by the James Beard Foundation for outstanding culinary writing across various categories.
Pulitzer Prize
Awarded annually by New York's Columbia University for excellence in fiction, drama, history, biography, poetry and general nonfiction.
National Book Award
Established in 1950 to celebrate the best writing in the United States. Awards are given to one book (author) annually in each of five categories: fiction, nonfiction, poetry, translation, and young people's literature.
Man Booker Prize
Awarded annually for best original novel written in English and published in the U.K., the Booker Prize is regarded as one of the most prestigious awards in literature.
Hugo Award
Voted as the best science fiction or fantasy works for the year by the World Science Fiction Convention.
CLEL Bell Picture Book Awards
The Colorado Libraries for Early Literacy (CLEL) awards five high-quality children's books that provide excellent support of early literacy development in young children. Awards are given in the categories for the five early literacy practices—read, write, sing, talk and play.
Michael L. Printz Award
Awarded annually by the Young Adult Library Services Association for excellence in young adult literature.
Edgar Award
Presented annually by the Mystery Writers of America. Named after Edgar Allan Poe, the awards honor the best in mystery fiction, non-fiction, television, film, and theater.
Library Reads
A monthly library staff picks list for adult fiction and non-fiction tiles chosen by librarians across the country.
ALSC Notable Children's Books
The Association for Library Service to Children (ALSC) recognizes the best in children's books from the past year, as chosen by their Notable Childrens Books Committee.
Newbery Medal
Awarded annually to the author of the most distinguished contribution to American literature for children. The Newbery and the Caldecott Medal are considered the two most prestigious awards for children's literature in the United States
Audie Awards
Recognizing distinction in audiobooks and spoken-word entertainment since 1996 by the Audio Publishers Association.
Boston Globe-Horn Book Award
Presented annually since 1967 by the Boston Globe and The Horn Book Magazine for excellence in children's and young adult literature.
CLEL Bell Picture Book Awards
The Colorado Libraries for Early Literacy (CLEL) awards five high-quality children's books that provide excellent support of early literacy development in young children. Awards are given in the categories for the five early literacy practices—read, write, sing, talk and play.
Coretta Scott King Award
Awarded annually since 1970 to authors and illustrators of books for children and young adults that demonstrate an appreciation of African-American culture.
Spanish CLEL Bell Book Awards
Modeled after the CLEL Bell Awards, this award recognizes Spanish-language books that effectively help caregivers, librarians, and other early childhood advocates support children's reading, writing, talking, playing, and singing skills.
Caldecott Medal
The Caldecott was named in honor of nineteenth-century English illustrator Randolph Caldecott. It is awarded annually by the ALSC to the artist of the most distinguished American picture book for children.
