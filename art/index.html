Community Art on Display

Douglas County Libraries supports the arts by featuring stunning public art and donating display space at select branches for local artists.

Interested in Exhibiting Your Work?

Castle Rock

The Douglas Land Conservancy Presents:

Capturing the Beauty of Open Land

Douglas Land Conservancy, a nonprofit land trust dedicated to preserving open space in and around Douglas County since 1987, presents “Capturing the Beauty of Open Land." Through oils, watercolors and photographs, Douglas Land Conservancy will transport you to places right outside your door! Come experience this breathtaking exhibit and immerse yourself in the beauty that is Douglas County.

Lone Tree

Sean McEniry

After a career in horticulture, Sean McEniry found that oil painting was an effective and fun outlet for his creative side. He is mostly self-taught and paints using oils and acrylics on canvas. His use of rich colors is a reflection of how he sees or wants to see the world.
Visit the Artist's Website »

Roxborough

Jim Bennett

Jim Bennett began taking pictures at about age nine, when he got his first box camera. High school photography classes increased his interest in photography. Jim moved to Colorado in 2006 and relishes the state’s endless opportunities and resources for landscape and nature photography. He belongs to the Roxborough Arts Council, Highlands Ranch Arts Guild, Gilpin County Arts Guild, and is president of the Highlands Ranch Photo Club.
