Why We Read

Summer reading is fun and playful — you can read anything you like! We encourage kids and adults alike to be creative with it and to make connections through entertaining events and activities.
Kids, print a reading log and then read for 720 minutes or complete fun alternate activities to finish the program and win prizes. Readers ages 13 and up can read for 900 minutes to win a limited edition DCL book bag. Print a log or track your minutes and finish online.
Help your school win a $250 prize in our new school contest. You can also help us reach our community reading goal of 7 million minutes. If we reach the goal, Douglas County Libraries Foundation will donate $500 to the Denver Dumb Friends League Buddy Center. Get reading!

Contest: School Treasure!

Help your school win some booty! It's easy — all you have to do is register for summer reading. The school with the most Summer Reading Program sign-ups by July 31 wins $250. Be sure to include your school name and your grade when registering online so your school gets credit. Winner will be announced in August. Fair winds, matey!

DCL Community Goal: 7 Million Minutes

Help us read for a cause this year. Once we reach 7 million minutes, the Douglas County Libraries Foundation will donate $500 to the Denver Dumb Friends League Buddy Center in Castle Rock to help our furry friends in need. Track your reading time online when you register for the reading program.

Quiz: Which Peter Pan Character Are You?

Who would you be in Never Never Land? Take our quiz to find out.
Contest applies only to K-12 students in schools affiliated with Douglas County School District. There will be one winning school at each level: elementary, middle and high. Sign-ups are tallied through Wandoo Reader online registration. The school at each level with the most sign-ups as a percentage of 2017-2018 enrollment wins. Contest runs May 1-July 31. Prizes courtesy of Douglas County Libraries Foundation. Winners will be notified in August 2018.
