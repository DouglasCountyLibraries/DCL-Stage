Adult Education

DCL offers nontraditional options for adults ages 17 and up to realize their high school education and career goals. Complete the form to get started. Questions or comments? Contact our Adult Literacy Team.
Citizenship & ESL
U.S. Citizenship
Interested candidates for U.S. citizenship can apply to receive tutoring help to prepare for the citizenship exam or attend a citizenship preparation class.
Apply Now
Practice Your English
Adults of all skill levels, cultures and language backgrounds converse in English in a relaxed, volunteer-facilitated environment. Held four times monthly at various Douglas County Libraries locations. Join the ESL book club for additional reading practice.

Tutoring & Proctoring
Adult Tutoring
Tutoring for adult English learners, basic literacy, and U.S. citizenship and GED/HiSET exam preparation is personalized to student needs. Adult English learners meet with tutors for one hour a week for three months. Citizenship students meet with tutors until they are ready to pass the test.
  • Application for an ESL or Citizenship Tutor
  • GED/HiSET or Adult Basic Literacy Tutoring

Tech Tutoring
We match customers to tutors for individualized learning in technology subjects like basic internet use, tablets, email, e-books, popular software basics and more. Request a one-hour weekday session with library staff now.
Request a Session

Education & Career Programs
Tech Tutoring at DCL
This is a one-on-one service where you can sit down with a trained library staff member to help you achieve your specific technology goal(s).
We can help with multiple tasks, including:
  • Basic Computer Skills:
    How to use a mouse and keyboard, the basics of a new device, tips on organizing your laptop.
  • Basic Mobile Phone Skills:
    Learn to use a VPN, create or use a Wi-Fi hotspot, manage security settings and organize your photos.
  • Basic Word Processing:
    Get started with Microsoft Word, Excel, or PowerPoint (or their equivalents), or help utilizing templates, organizing a page, or making edits to existing documents.
  • Downloading Apps and E-books:
    Get assistance with a Kindle or other e-reader devices, and learn the basics of using the Libby or Hoopla apps.
  • Internet Safety:
    Get basic tips, suggested resources, and best practices for keeping your accounts and devices safe, including how to recognize and avoid malware, viruses, spam and phishing attempts.
  • Email:
    Set up a free email account of choice, and learn to send/receive attachments safely and keep your email organized.
  • Social Media Basics:
    Learn about the different types of social media platforms and get help setting up an account, or just get answers to your general questions about social media.
  • DCL Database Basics:
    Discover the various research databases accessible at DCL, such as Consumer Reports, Legal Forms, Investing Center, MyHeritage and many more.
Proctoring Guidelines
  • Proctoring services are provided by appointment only. We do not offer walk-in or same day testing.
  • We are unable to provide continuous monitoring of testing. Please check your school or institution's monitoring requirements before completing a proctoring request.
  • We must receive written confirmation of acceptance of our guidelines from your school/institution or receive proctor approval from your institution.
  • We must receive exam materials 48 hours in advance of a testing appointment.
  • The proctor will provide a computer for any online testing. To ensure access to the exam, please bring your login credentials to your appointment.
  • Secure storage is not available for personal items. Please do not bring a backpack, purse, bag, mobile phone or other smart devices to your testing appointment.
  • Please bring a current photo ID to each exam appointment. A digital ID is not an acceptable proof of identification for testing.
  • We can scan/email completed exam materials to your school or testing institution. Students are responsible for all costs for returning exams by mail.
  • Proctors cannot provide any personal identification numbers as part of the proctor approval process.
