High School Diploma

Career Online High School (COHS)

COHS is an accredited online high school diploma and career certification program designed to help adults ages 19 and up realize their educational and career goals.
Learn More

High School Equivalency Test Preparation

Students may attend any of three weekly opportunities to prepare for equivalency testing: Mondays and Wednesdays at 6 p.m. in Parker, and Tuesdays at 6 p.m. in Castle Rock. Prepare for the GED, HiSET or TASC exam.

Citizenship & ESL

U.S. Citizenship

Candidates for U.S. citizenship can choose from two classes a year (three sessions each) to prepare for citizenship application and interviews. Representatives from USCIS attend to answer your questions.
View Calendar

Practice Your English

Adults of all skill levels, cultures and language backgrounds converse in English in a relaxed environment, facilitated by trained volunteers. Practice Your English is held Saturdays at 10:30 a.m. at Douglas County Libraries in Highlands Ranch and Parker.

Tutoring & Proctoring

Adult Tutoring

Tutoring for ESL, basic literacy, and high school equivalency exam preparation is personalized to individual student needs. ESL and basic literacy students meet with tutors one hour a week for six months; high school equivalency students study with a tutor for three months.

Proctoring

We are pleased to offer free test proctoring at four locations, by appointment. Some restrictions apply.

Proctoring Guidelines

  • Before a proctoring appointment may be made, we must have received written confirmation from your school that it accepts our guidelines and/or we must have approved your school's proctoring guidelines.
  • Proctors are unable to stay in the room with you when you test. We cannot store valuables for you.
  • Unless you specified that your exam must be proctored by someone with a Master of Library Science degree, we may provide a proctor without an MLS.
  • Please make sure your exam reaches your assigned proctor at least 48 hours before your proctoring appointment.
  • You must present a current photo ID before each exam. Minors who do not have a photo ID must be accompanied by a parent with a photo ID.
  • Please be advised that requests from the school for a staff member's personal identification will be declined. Examples of personal identification numbers that will not be provided include Social Security number (even if the request is only for the last four digits), driver's license number, and employee number.
  • We can scan and email completed exams to your school, free of charge. You are responsible for any costs, including postage and an envelope, for returning the completed exam by mail.
