High School Diploma

Career Online High School (COHS)

COHS is an accredited online high school diploma and career certification program designed to help adults ages 19 and up realize their educational and career goals.
Learn More

High School Equivalency Test Preparation

Students may attend any of two weekly opportunities to prepare for equivalency testing: Mondays at 6 p.m. in Parker, and Tuesdays at 6 p.m. in Castle Rock. Prepare for the GED, HiSET or TASC exam.

Citizenship & ESL

U.S. Citizenship

Candidates for U.S. citizenship can choose from two classes a year (three sessions each) to prepare for citizenship application and interviews. Representatives from USCIS attend to answer your questions.
View Calendar

Practice Your English

Adults of all skill levels, cultures and language backgrounds converse in English in a relaxed environment, facilitated by trained volunteers. Practice Your English is held Saturdays at 10:30 a.m. at Douglas County Libraries in Highlands Ranch and Parker.

Tutoring & Proctoring

Adult Tutoring

Tutoring for ESL, basic literacy, and high school equivalency exam preparation is personalized to individual student needs. ESL and basic literacy students meet with tutors one hour a week for six months; high school equivalency students study with a tutor for three months.

Proctoring

We are pleased to offer free test proctoring at four locations, by appointment. By submitting the form, I agree to DCL's Proctoring Guidelines.

Proctoring Guidelines

  • Our proctoring team must receive written confirmation from your school that it accepts our guidelines and/or we must have approved your school’s proctoring guidelines.
  • Proctors are unable to stay in the room with you when you test. We cannot provide secure storage for any items not allowed with the test taker.
  • We cannot guarantee that the proctor for your exam has an MLS degree.
  • Please make sure your exam reaches your assigned proctor at least 48 hours prior to your proctoring appointment.
  • Test takers must provide a current photo ID before each exam. A parent or legal guardian with a photo ID must accompany minors who are not able to provide their own photo ID.
  • Proctors are not able to provide your school with personal identification numbers, such as Social Security numbers (including the last four digits), driver's license numbers, or employee ID numbers.
  • We can scan and email completed exams to your school, free of charge. The student is responsible for any costs, including postage and an envelope, if returning the exam by mail. We are not able to fax exams.
