Welcome to Douglas County Libraries!

Douglas County Libraries (DCL) volunteers are integral partners to serving our community. On any day, at any branch, volunteers are part of our team. Volunteers, by their generosity, reflect the humanism that exists within each community. We value and appreciate the time, talent and expertise you share at our libraries. Approximately 1,500 thoughtful citizens volunteer each year at our seven branches, contributing an amazing 40,000 volunteer hours.
Volunteers and staff comprise One DCL, in which together we create community impact, uphold our bold vision, and offer unparalleled service to our community. In addition, volunteers are our ambassadors in the community. When you share your stories about volunteering at DCL, you engage your friends, family and neighbors with our organization and pique their curiosity. You carry our message to them about why our vision is so important.
One of the most thoughtful gifts you can give anyone is your time. You chose us as a way to give back to your community, and for that we are forever grateful. I look forward to having you join our premium volunteer team. And I'm excited to learn how your volunteerism at DCL changes your own life, too.
Let us involve you in elevating our community by inspiring a love of reading, discovery and connection. Thank you for joining our team. You are key to our success in serving our community!
Sincerely,
Ali Ayres
Department Head Volunteer Services

Our Vision

Douglas County Libraries elevates our community by inspiring a love of reading, discovery and connection.

Our Core Values

It is difficult to achieve an amazing library without good structure to do our jobs; great collections to tempt our customers; solid, contemporary technologies; customers voting with their feet about the success of our programs and services; and the resources to carry the whole thing off.
Our core values are practical, necessary and flawed. Without a culture based on values—aspirations that define our relationships to each other and our customers—the library ingredients don't matter very much. Our values see us through good days and bad. Strategies come and go. The following values reflect DCL's brand.
We are Welcoming to All. We strive to make any interaction with us personal and premium, and to make a positive impact. Our priority is service to those we are privileged
"We wish to translate our values into action in our dealings with each other and our customers."
Bob Pasicznyuk, Library Director
to call customers and coworkers. We deliver more than is expected through surprising, thoughtful choices.
We value Continual Growth. We work through challenges with a positive attitude, value differences of thought, opinion and worldview, and create positive energy by focusing on creativity and possibility. We strive to exceed our community's expectations and lean forward to serve.
We value Authentic Relationships. We earn trust every day through competence, character and connection. We are intentional in our work, and help each other succeed. We strive to be the "best of us" so that we can give the "best of us."

Service Enterprise

DCL is proud to be Service Enterprise Certified, a designation that signifies our commitment to best practices for engaging volunteers. The national Service Enterprise Initiative, led by Points of Light, helps nonprofit organizations leverage volunteers and their skills to address community needs. Through Service Enterprise (SE), organizations identify the key steps to build volunteer engagement and fully utilize the skills of volunteers, maximizing their potential. SE organizations are known to be more adaptable, sustainable and capable. For example, every $1 invested in volunteer engagement at DCL yields $5 in return, a 500% return on volunteer investment.
DCL was certified in December, 2017, in the first cohort for Colorado. We were the fifth organization to be certified in our state. In the year following our SE certification, we experienced a 15% increase in volunteer hours; in the second year, a 25% increase.
DCL pursued SE certification because it strongly aligns with our core values and goals: who we are and how we behave. While we work to elevate our community, our volunteers elevate us through their gift of time and talent.

Our Collection

DCL is the fourth-largest library system in Colorado with nearly 2 million visitors annually. We play a vital role in the life of our community with programs and services for kids, teens and adults. We lend nearly 7 million books, DVDs, magazines, e-books and audiobooks every year. We also have nontraditional items, such as Oculus Go virtual reality headsets, karaoke systems, programmable robots, Go Pros, Nikon cameras, Super Nintendo systems, sewing machines, and wireless hotspots, just to name a few. Our participation with Prospector and Interlibrary Loan ensures that library customers around the country enjoy our amazing collection.

Benefits of Volunteering in Your Community

"Giving to others can help protect your mental and physical health. It can reduce stress, combat depression, keep you mentally stimulated and provide a sense of purpose. While it's true that the more you volunteer, the more benefits you'll experience, volunteering doesn't have to involve a long-term commitment or take a huge amount of time out of your busy day. Giving even in simple ways can help those in need and improve your health and happiness."

DCL volunteers report these and other benefits from their experience at DCL:
  • Making new friends
  • Enhanced sense of purpose
  • Staying active
  • Feeling valued and appreciated
  • Feeling like they make a difference
When you volunteer with us, you join a team of dedicated people who are willing to give their time and effort toward a great cause: helping their local library continue to be the vibrant heart of their community. DCL is tremendously fortunate to have hundreds of amazing volunteers serving our community alongside us. We are excited to welcome you!

Volunteer Connect Douglas County

Volunteer Connect is an easy, one-stop site for volunteers seeking ways to get involved in Douglas County. The website is an initiative of the Partnership of Douglas County Governments, established in 2014 to sustain a culture of volunteerism in our local community. We post most DCL volunteer opportunities on the site.
Use the website to find opportunities that match your passion by filtering choices and narrowing down the list of available opportunities. You can read about each opportunity to learn the details, time and location. We look forward to learning which position best matches you. We recommend that you visit this website often to see new opportunities listed.
If you do not find the perfect match, please call or email our Volunteer Services staff for additional ways to be involved.

Rights & Responsibilities

Rights
DCL provides volunteers with a safe, nondiscriminatory environment that complies with federal laws for Equal Employment Opportunities (EEO), the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and guidelines for a drug-free and harassment-free environment while performing assigned tasks. DCL staff will provide all necessary supervision and training.
Responsibilities
We ask you to:
  • Act in accordance with the guidance and decisions of library staff and stay within the bounds of volunteer responsibilities.
  • Act courteously to customers and employees. Professional conduct is important, as is customer privacy. Please refrain from discussing any customer by name or discussing any customer's reading selections with anyone.
  • Please report on time for your scheduled shift. We are counting on your volunteerism for the success of our events and daily operations. If you are unable to keep your commitment it is critical that you inform staff as soon as possible.
  • Wear a volunteer nametag at all times while in the library.
  • Please record the number of hours volunteered after each shift. Having volunteer data helps us to grow our program.
  • Dress neatly and appropriately: jeans are fine as long as they do not have holes; shirts must cover your waist. Closed-toe shoes are recommended for safety. Our libraries are fragrance-free, so please refrain from wearing scented products.
  • Put your cell phone on silent mode and keep cell phone use to a minimum. A brief emergency call is acceptable.

Roles

We are grateful to work with volunteers of all ages and abilities! Whether you are a community volunteer seeking to give back or a teen working on your community service hours to graduate, we are pleased to have you volunteer your time at Douglas County Libraries.
In addition, we welcome people who are fulfilling court-ordered community service for a misdemeanor crime. The purpose behind this service stems from the concept of "repaying" the community for a crime committed. Many individuals learn from their mistakes in this capacity while also creating a new connection with their community.
Volunteer roles vary widely at DCL. General opportunities include: shelving, event help, birthday parties, used bookstore and online book sales help, homebound delivery, Storytime help, cleaning discs in the collection, grooming and more. In addition, we offer opportunities to volunteer in our Archives & Local History department located in the Castle Rock branch. Most of our shifts range from 1-4 hours with appropriate breaks.
Special teen opportunities include reading with young buddies to boost their self-confidence, Teen Library Council to plan teen events, and helping at many signature events and programs that our seven branches offer year-round.
Unique and rewarding literacy-focused opportunities include sharing early literacy stories and activities with children at early learning centers or in-home daycare providers; chaperoning when volunteers' trained dogs offer a listening ear to young, struggling readers; and serving as a tutor to help adults learn English, prepare for high school equivalency exams, and celebrate cultural literacy.
Court-ordered volunteers assist library staff with removing materials that are no longer circulating, grooming, checking the contents of audiovisual items, and other duties as assigned.

Workplace Policies

Established organizational policies provide clear expectations and standards for all and are helpful in connecting our core values to our daily practices. Essential policies are described here; follow the links for expanded information.
Youth Volunteer Policy
  • Signed parent/guardian permission slips are required for anyone 13 years of age and younger.
  • 13-year-olds and older may volunteer at our branches without an adult present and with the Branch Volunteer Coordinator's verbal consent after meeting the volunteer.
  • 12-year-olds and younger must volunteer with a parent or guardian present who is at least 18 years old.
  • 12-year-olds and younger who are part of a group volunteering together (Scouts, book valet) must be accompanied by a group leader at a ratio of 1 group leader per 8 volunteers. The group leader must be 21 years of age or older.
  • Youth Volunteer Permission Form
Dress Policy
Please dress neatly and appropriately. Remember, you are volunteering in a public place so do not wear revealing clothing or shirts with potentially offensive messages. Make sure shirts cover your waist when you bend, and no short shorts. Jeans are fine as long as they do not have holes. In addition, keep in mind that you will often be doing physical work, so dress comfortably. Closed-toed shoes are recommended. Our libraries are fragrance-free environments. Please refrain from wearing scented products.
Cell Phone Policy
DCL recognizes that phone usage is an integral part of our environment. In the interest of good business practice, phone calls and texting must not interfere with your volunteer duties.
Personal use of library phones for long distance is not permitted, unless for library business reasons. Excessive personal calls or texting during the day, regardless of the phone used, can interfere with productivity and is distracting to others.
Volunteers are asked to make personal calls on non-volunteer time and to ensure that your friends and family members are aware of DCL's procedures. Flexibility will be provided in circumstances demanding immediate attention.
Fragrance in the Workplace
The District strives to ensure a healthy and safe workplace environment, and recognizes that employees, volunteers and visitors to our facilities may react to fragrant products. Due to health concerns arising from exposure to scented products, DCL is continually working to ensure that products used in the operation of its facilities are fragrance-free and follow best practices to limit employee, volunteer and customer exposure to chemicals.
DCL encourages employees and volunteers to be cautious in the personal products they utilize. Personal products (fragrances, colognes, lotions, powders and other similar products) worn by employees and volunteers should not be overly perceptible to others.
DCL reserves the right to identify work areas as "fragrance free" and to require employees and volunteers to not wear fragrant products to accommodate others with chemical sensitivity. Any employee or volunteer with a concern about scents or odors should contact a Human Resources representative or Branch Volunteer Coordinator.

Recognition & Reward

"How wonderful it is that nobody need wait a single moment before starting to improve the world."
Anne Frank
Although the rewards of volunteering are often intrinsic, it's important to DCL to provide recognition and rewards to make sure you know how much we value you.
In honor of your service, we provide recognition in a variety of ways:
  • DCL is a Certified Organization for the national and prestigious Presidential Volunteer Service Award. We are proud to honor many of our volunteers with this award each year.
  • Library bookstore discounts. Volunteer Bonus Cards are issued after 10 volunteer hours are achieved. You have unlimited use of this card to buy one item and get a second item of equal or lesser value free for an entire calendar year. New cards are issued annually.
  • We host annual volunteer appreciation events at each branch, where you can socialize with library staff and fellow branch volunteers.
  • We also host an annual districtwide volunteer appreciation event, where volunteers and staff from around the district spend a fun evening together.

Discontinuing Your Volunteer Role

While we are grateful for your volunteerism, we understand that sometimes volunteers need to end their commitment to the library. If you will be discontinuing your volunteer activities either temporarily or permanently, a two-week notice is preferred to find a replacement for your shift. In addition, when you discontinue your service to the library, please return your nametag.

Discipline

The District understands that occasionally performance or other behaviors may not comply with our procedures or may fall short of our expectations. Disciplinary actions can range from a formal discussion with the volunteer about the matter to immediate termination. Action taken by management in an individual case does not establish a precedent in other circumstances.

Safety & Wellness

DCL Safety Policy

The District is committed to a safe work environment for employees and volunteers. It is important to recognize that safety at work is everyone's responsibility. Each individual should ensure that all tasks are conducted in a safe and efficient manner, complying with all local, state and federal safety and health regulations.
Safety begins with the way we think about our roles. Doing work safely promotes safe work practices for everyone. Employees and volunteers should report any unsafe practices or conditions to a supervisor.
COVID-19 or Other Contagions
COVID-19 has prompted us to think carefully about how we interact with one another with regard to the spread of viruses. We ask that volunteers exercise extreme caution for their own safety and that of others. If you volunteer during an outbreak, please notify us if you have any symptoms, concerns, or are in a high-risk group. DCL will require staff and volunteers to wear face masks for everyone's protection.
Similarly, it is critical to maintain the safety of our work areas and that you wipe down all equipment to prevent potential viral spread. Your volunteer supervisor will provide you with the needed supplies.
Emergency Evacuation
In the event of an emergency or fire alarm, please leave through the nearest exit and follow staff directions. We will be informed when the building is safe for re-entry.
Snow Days
Your safety is our number-one priority. DCL will post closures and late opening notices at DCL.org and a recorded message at (303) 791-7323. If the library is open and you are concerned about driving in poor weather, please use your best judgment and be sure to notify us if you cannot keep your scheduled shift.
Working Safely With Materials and Tools
DCL makes every effort to use chemicals appropriate to the job with the least health and environmental risks. Users of the chemical are responsible for using the chemical as directed and to know the risks associated with the chemical's use. Material Safety Data Sheets (SDS) were developed to support your right to know. An SDS contains technical, health and emergency first-aid information for each chemical, provided by the manufacturer. These documents are available from the Branch Volunteer Coordinator.
Safety Skill Guides
The health and safety of paid and unpaid staff members is of great importance to DCL. The prevention of accidents and injuries takes precedence over expedience. In the conduct of our business, every attempt will be made to ensure a healthy and safe environment and to prevent accidents. We offer the following resources so you can be proactive and careful about any physical tasks that your volunteerism involves.
